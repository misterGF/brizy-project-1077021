Common Digital Transformation Mistakes To Avoid

Regularly assessing the growth of your organization is key to its success – but as a business leader, you already know and practice that.

However, it has become difficult to ascertain what the future holds, especially since the pandemic.

Ask yourself: have your strategies altered? Have your consumer demands changed? Is the business still growing strategically? Today, the ability to align products, operations, and implementation with a predominantly customer-centric market determines the success of your efforts.

Especially in the times of COVID-19, learning to conduct businesses digitally has become a necessary skill. This is particularly binding for all startups and aspiring entrepreneurs looking to make a mark.

Digital transformation is the future, and it is key for new businesses to make this transformation as seamlessly as possible.

A great example of how digital platforms can allow you to sustain and grow your business is the National Basketball Association. With the cancellation of the entire season due to the pandemic, the NBA lost a major part of their television and ad revenue.

However, to keep up their revenues, they filmed public service announcements sharing vital health information with their fans, helping keep their spirits up.

They have since garnered 40 million views and are depending entirely on social media for their revenue. Interestingly, the NBA has also managed to remain on the top of their audience’s minds even though they haven’t had a game in several months now.However, not everyone’s had such resounding success. Many businesses continue to struggle with their websites, digital logistics, security, and other issues simply because they failed to first invest in expert resources.

5 Digital Transformation Mistakes To Avoid

Devoid of proper guidance, it can be pretty difficult to meet your objective of being technologically savvy.Here are the five ways businesses can mess up setting up for a digital transformation:

1 - Sticking Only To In-House Resources

In one major travel company, the IT department embarked on an in-house digitalization initiative only to realize that they lack expertise in critical areas like software development and AI technology. The company was equipped to handle small-scale digital projects, but not a full-scale permanent digitalization.

Businesses need people who have a multi-dimensional outlook: they not only have a sound grip on how things are done but are also aware of operational realities and concerns of the business. For this reason, it is important to bring in outside resources that can enhance the impact of your digital transformation.

In-house resources can be directed towards achieving high-priority tasks associated with digital transformation. At the same time, external partners can take care of strategies and execution programs.

Usually, it’s a good idea to hire tech consultants who are equipped, experienced, and can help devise a strategic digitalization plan.

2 - Not Valuing Communication And Co-Creation

Sometimes there is a gap between the C-executives and the AB-executives when implementing a growth strategy. The Board of Directors must make their vision and purpose of the project clear to the management to avoid any confusion.

Identifying the main influencers in expanding the business is also the key. For instance, when transforming your business digitally, the IT department plays a huge role. Since they understand the use of customers,

employees, and the use of software, their suggestions during planning can be of remarkable assistance.

Successful growth is possible only if it’s balanced across all segments of the company.

3 - Compromising On Manpower

Often, when businesses go through a digital transformation as part of their growth strategy, they tend to overlook the problem of staffing. Even the right technology won’t deliver fruitful results if the staff isn’t trained to handle it.

And that’s the problem in a nutshell. Businesses often fail to realize that the new tech is only as good as the people using it. So, map out the specialized skills that each department will require and recruit accordingly. Provide appropriate tools to your employees to collaborate, share, and access any information that will help in serving your customers

better.

Additionally, you can also invest in training programs led by an independent digital expert to help you cultivate the human capital required for success.

4 - Failure To Understand Changing Customer Needs

From the very beginning, it is essential to navigate customer needs as they change with time, consequently aligning your business’s aim with that of your customers.

When we talk about digitalizing a business, it should be an immaculate process. From a seamless online shopping experience to a secured payment and timely delivery system, your ability to generate value for your customers determines your success.

  • Make use of digital technology like mobile location data, online surveys, and sensory data from the phone to gather deep customer insights.
  • Omni-channeling your business, i.e., allowing your consumer to access your business from multiple channels and devices, making use of technologies like mobile, cloud, and APIs.
  •  Design efficient communication channels between customers and employees, including an online efficient complaint system.

It is critical that this reconceptualization of the also considers the end-to-end delivery of the service.

5 - Unorganized Planning And Leadership

As the company embarks on a journey to achieve its goals, improper planning and leadership can set the company up for failure. The first step to planning is to ask yourself, “What is your end-goal?” Then work

backward and try to align your strategies with your objectives.

This strategy has helped many successful companies digitize without breaking the bank or causing disruptions in the workflow. The Head of Strategy for IoT at Lenovo, Todd DeSaix says, “setting a clear goal and an end in mind is a key step in avoiding digital transformation failures.”

Make sure to devise a strategic plan for each department, set KPI’s and bonus-drivers to keep your business incentive-driven.

In Conclusion

By now, you must have realized that whether you are starting in the industry or merely looking to transform your business digitally, efficient strategic planning is the key to success.
